What is the Ethiopian Calendar

The Ethiopian calendar, also known as the Ge'ez calendar, is the principal calendar used in Ethiopia. It has 12 months of 30 days each and a 13th month called Pagumē with 5 days (6 in leap years). The Ethiopian calendar is approximately 7-8 years behind the Gregorian calendar.

This unique calendar system is based on the ancient Coptic calendar and reflects Ethiopia's rich cultural heritage and independent historical development.
